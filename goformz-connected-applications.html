Overview

Most third-party app integrations in GoFormz are managed via app connections. An app connection allows GoFormz to communicate with the third-party app. To establish an app connection, you must provide GoFormz with the credentials for logging into the third-party app, which typically include username and password, but may also include app-specific information like a custom subdomain. Once you have established an app connection, you can use workflows to build integrations between GoFormz and your app.

My Role

Senior Product Designer
User Research, Interaction, Visual design, Prototyping & testing, Front-end development

The Problem

Integrations are tremendosly profitable part of our business but user adoption is low.

The existing integration platform lacks discovery and a self-explanatory UI. Users have to call their account manager to setup an integration, and once the integration is activated setup is tedious.

My objective was to redesign connected apps for the SMB user and redesign webhooks for the technical user.

User Profiles

This is a two part product for two users. It was difficult to keep the experience unified as one user has less technical experience than the other.

Office Manager

Goals and objectives
  • Wants to discover different applications they can integrate their GoFormz with
  • Wants to automatically have completed forms be saved to their Box account

Web Admin

Goals and Objectives
  • Needs to be able to see that webhooks were successfully fired
  • If a webhook misfires, they need to be able diagnose and fixx the problem.

Analyzing the current product

I sat next to a customer that asked how they can connect their account to Box. The walk though was frustrating to the user. Once they finally found the connected apps screen they didn't know what to do when they landed there. It was just a list of apps we have with no clear descriptions of how that application be integrated with their GoFormz account.

Assessing the landscape and creating wireframes

Though this product is fairly straight forward it was important to get the wireframes right. I wanted Connected Apps to follow similar patterns across other companies. Identify the problem, locate the appropriate appication to address this problem, and quickly setup an integration to fix the problem.


The solution: An easy-to-use app marketplace

Not just a list of applications with vauge descriptions. We now have a full marketplace with detailed pages that give the user the ability to discover and manage their integrations.


A cleaner more accessible interface

We added a brief description of what connected apps can do for your business. It was then important for the users to see and edit existing connections. Below existing connections we added a grid of available applications.

In the detail screen users have access to content marketing where they can first learn more about the integration if they want to add it. They can also see all existing workflows for that integration.

Webhooks and APIs

After the integration is setup it was crucial that a developer could locate and fix the webhook if it misfired.


The Webhook UI needed to have the following:
  • Metadata
  • Number of attempted connections, when they succeed and when they fail
  • The ability to see the targeted URL the failed payload.

Project Learnings

Discoverability is important

Connected apps is expensive but useful to our customers. If they can discover usecases on their own they don't have to be upsold into an integration by an account manager. It becomes a nautral transition that moves the user upward through the GoFormz ecosystem and increases their lifetime value.
